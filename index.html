Growth Strategy

How a community is actually built

Starting point: zero audience, zero visibility, zero existing sketching culture in the city.

The strategy wasn't to build a following — it was to create a recurring shared experience that people wanted to be part of. Events drove discovery. Identity made people stay. The global network gave it legitimacy.

Brand Identity

Visual identity built
from scratch

Brand identity was developed to reflect the community's values: direct observation, honest drawing, shared presence. The visual language — black liner sketch style with orange accents — was applied consistently across digital content, print materials, merchandise, and event production.

The manifesto wasn't written by me — it belongs to the global Urban Sketchers movement. But bringing it to life visually, and making it feel local and owned, was the brand challenge.

Community Insight

A community doesn't grow when you post more. It grows when people have something to show up for.

Every growth spike correlated with an event — not a post, not a campaign. The content was evidence of the experience, not a substitute for it.

This is directly applicable to developer community building: documentation, release notes, and forum activity matter — but nothing replaces a live event, a real conversation, or a shared creative act. The medium is the meeting.
